## Book Description (paste into KDP; ~3,200 characters incl. HTML)

<b>Most NCMHCE prep gives you tables. The exam gives you a human being in crisis — and four answers that all look right.</b>

The NCMHCE doesn't test whether you can recite DSM criteria. In the case-simulation format, the diagnosis is usually handed to you. What's scored is everything that comes after: what you assess first, what you rule out, what you say next, and which evidence-based treatment you choose. Candidates who fail rarely fail on knowledge — they fail on clinical prioritization under pressure.

<b>The Complete NCMHCE Study Guide</b> was built around that reality. Instead of terse reference tables, every diagnostic family gets a full teaching chapter that shows you how the exam actually tests it — and how to think your way to the scored answer.

<b>Inside you'll find:</b>

<ul>
<li><b>The Universal Priority Ladder</b> — the seven-rung decision framework (safety → medical rule-outs → stabilization → alliance → assessment → treatment → ethics) that resolves nearly every "two answers look right" dilemma</li>
<li><b>All 18 diagnostic families, 92 diagnoses</b> — each with quick-reference tables (ICD-10 codes, first-line treatments) framed by narrative discussion</li>
<li><b>"The Clinical Picture"</b> — prose descriptions of how each disorder actually presents inside a case simulation, not just criteria lists</li>
<li><b>Differentials with the deciding feature</b> — MDD vs. bipolar depression, BPD vs. Bipolar II, OCD vs. OCPD, schizoid vs. avoidant, delirium vs. dementia — every look-alike pair, with the one feature that settles it</li>
<li><b>"What the Exam Rewards / What It Punishes"</b> — the scored priorities and the classic traps in every chapter, each with the reason why</li>
<li><b>Worked mini-cases</b> — full reasoning chains from vignette to answer, including why each distractor loses</li>
<li><b>Assessment tools in context</b> — PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5, AUDIT, C-SSRS and dozens more, with cutoff scores and when/why to use each</li>
<li><b>60 practice questions</b> with expanded rationales, plus a complete answer key</li>
<li><b>Foundations that get tested</b> — therapeutic alliance and rupture repair, theory pattern-matching shortcuts, the microskills hierarchy, group work, and a full worked case walkthrough with scored decision points</li>
<li><b>Clinical quick reference</b> — defense mechanisms, cognitive distortions, and a complete assessment-tool compendium</li>
</ul>

<b>Written for the 2022+ exam format.</b> Whether you're weeks out from your test date or just starting to prepare, this guide teaches the <i>why</i> behind every answer — so when the case simulation puts a client in front of you, you'll know exactly what to do first.

Your license is waiting on the other side of this exam. Prepare like a clinician, not a memorizer.
