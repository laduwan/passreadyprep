## Defense Mechanisms Quick Reference

Defense mechanisms are unconscious psychological processes that protect the ego from anxiety. The NCMHCE tests your ability to identify them in case vignettes — often as part of a psychodynamic conceptualization question.

### Mature Defenses *(adaptive; common in healthy adults)*

| Mechanism | Definition | Example on Exam |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Sublimation** | Channeling unacceptable impulses into socially acceptable outlets | A client with aggressive urges becomes a competitive athlete |
| **Humor** | Using comedy to manage uncomfortable feelings without denial | A client jokes about their diagnosis in a way that allows perspective-taking |
| **Altruism** | Relieving one's own distress by helping others | A client who experienced childhood poverty becomes a social worker |
| **Suppression** | Consciously setting aside a distressing thought to deal with later | A client decides not to think about the diagnosis until after the presentation |

*How it's tested:* A vignette describes a client who "channels his anger into competitive sports" — this is sublimation. The exam may ask you to identify it or distinguish it from displacement.

---

### Neurotic (Intermediate) Defenses *(less adaptive; common in neurotic range disorders)*

| Mechanism | Definition | Example on Exam |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Repression** | Unconscious exclusion of distressing material from awareness | Client has no memory of traumatic childhood events |
| **Reaction Formation** | Expressing the opposite of an unacceptable feeling | A client who feels rage toward a parent becomes excessively devoted |
| **Displacement** | Redirecting an impulse from the true target to a safer one | Client yells at her children after a bad day with her boss |
| **Rationalization** | Creating logical justifications for unacceptable feelings or behaviors | Client who overspends: "I work hard; I deserve nice things" |
| **Intellectualization** | Using abstract thinking to distance from emotional content | A client discusses grief using clinical terminology without showing affect |
| **Undoing** | Performing an action to "undo" a transgression | Client feels guilty about a thought and then completes a ritual to cancel it |
| **Isolation of Affect** | Separating thoughts from their associated emotions | Client recounts a traumatic event in complete detail with no emotional response |

*How it's tested:* Intellectualization frequently appears in cases where a client describes a loss using clinical or distant language. Reaction formation appears in cases where the stated feeling and the behavioral evidence are directly opposite.

---

### Immature Defenses *(maladaptive; common in personality disorders, severe pathology)*

| Mechanism | Definition | Example on Exam |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Denial** | Refusing to acknowledge a painful reality | Client with a serious medical diagnosis says "I don't believe there's really anything wrong" |
| **Projection** | Attributing one's own unacceptable feelings to others | Client says "everyone here is judging me" when she is the one judging herself |
| **Splitting** | Seeing people as all-good or all-bad; inability to tolerate ambivalence | BPD client idealizes the counselor one week, devalues the next |
| **Regression** | Returning to an earlier developmental stage under stress | An adult client begins speaking in a childlike voice when discussing abuse |
| **Somatization** | Converting psychological distress into physical symptoms | Client develops unexplained stomach pain before difficult family events |
| **Projective Identification** | Causing others to actually feel what you are projecting | BPD client subtly behaves in ways that cause the counselor to feel rage — then attributes the rage to the counselor |
| **Passive Aggression** | Expressing hostility indirectly | Client "forgets" appointments consistently when angry at the counselor |
| **Acting Out** | Expressing internal conflict through action rather than words | Client gets into a bar fight after a session where trauma was discussed |

*How it's tested:* Splitting is almost always tested in the context of BPD. Projection and projective identification may appear together in personality disorder questions. Acting out appears in cases where the client's behavior outside sessions reflects internal session material.

## Cognitive Distortions Quick Reference

Cognitive distortions are errors in thinking identified in CBT (Beck) that maintain depression, anxiety, and other conditions. The exam tests your ability to identify them in client statements.

| Distortion | Definition | Client Statement Example |
|-----------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **All-or-nothing thinking** (black-and-white) | Seeing things in extreme categories | "If I'm not perfect at this, I'm a complete failure." |
| **Catastrophizing** | Predicting the worst possible outcome | "If I fail this test, my life is over." |
| **Mind reading** | Assuming knowledge of others' thoughts | "I know she thinks I'm stupid." |
| **Fortune telling** | Predicting a negative future as fact | "I'll never be able to manage on my own." |
| **Emotional reasoning** | Assuming feelings reflect reality | "I feel worthless, so I must be worthless." |
| **Personalization** | Blaming oneself for events beyond control | "My parents divorced because of me." |
| **Overgeneralization** | Drawing broad conclusions from single events | "I made one mistake — I always mess everything up." |
| **Mental filter** | Focusing exclusively on one negative detail | The client hears universally positive feedback and fixates on one mild critique |
| **Discounting the positive** | Dismissing positive experiences as not counting | "The promotion doesn't count — they just felt sorry for me." |
| **Should statements** | Rigid rules about self/others | "I should be able to handle this without help." |
| **Labeling** | Attaching a global negative label to self or others | "I'm just a loser." "She's completely toxic." |
| **Magnification/Minimization** | Amplifying negatives; shrinking positives | "My one strength doesn't matter. My one failure defines me." |

### Restructuring Context

The goal of cognitive restructuring is not to replace negative thoughts with positive ones — it is to replace distorted thoughts with more accurate, evidence-based ones. The Socratic questioning sequence:

1. **What is the evidence for and against this thought?**
2. **What would you tell a friend who had this thought?**
3. **Is this thought helping you or hurting you?**
4. **What is a more accurate way to describe this situation?**

*Exam tip:* The exam will present a client statement and ask you to identify the distortion, or present a counselor response and ask whether it correctly targets the distortion. Know the distortions by both definition and example.

## Complete Answer Key with Expanded Rationales

### Questions 1–20

**Q1: B** — Client signals rupture through disengagement; first response is to explore the rupture, not defend treatment or assess symptoms. Moving to the relationship is the priority over any technique.

**Q2: B** — Cultural goals rupture; autonomy-focused treatment conflicts with collectivist values. Disengagement is communication about misaligned goals.

**Q3: B** — UPR validates experience without evaluating the decision. A compares to norms (evaluative). C seeks a "better option" (evaluative). D names concern (conditional).

**Q4: C** — Pre-termination regression is expected; naming the pattern is the first clinical response. Extension and consultation are possible later steps, not first responses.

**Q5: B** — Scaling questions with "what would make it a 7" is signature SFBT. MI uses scaling but not this specific extension pattern.

**Q6: B** — Enactment (structural family therapy): therapist observes family members interacting in session.

**Q7: C** — Externalization ("the depression has been trying to convince you") is signature narrative therapy.

**Q8: C** — REBT targets the irrational belief (B), not the activating event (A) or the consequences (C).

**Q9: C** — Immediacy response connects in-the-room behavior (apology) to an interpersonal pattern.

**Q10: C** — Empathic reflection holds ambivalence; alliance is still forming, so influencing skills (B, D) are premature.

**Q11: C** — Confrontation of an incongruity: today's nonverbal presentation vs. the usual presentation.

**Q12: B** — Brief, genuine, client-focused disclosure. A overclaims understanding. C breaches confidentiality. D is counselor-centered.

**Q13: C** — Storming stage conflict is therapeutic material; name and facilitate, don't deflect.

**Q14: B** — Brief empathic reflection + redirect to the group; balances warmth and responsibility to the group.

**Q15: C** — Instillation of hope: observing another member's recovery provides evidence that change is possible.

**Q16: B** — Accurately distinguishes counselor's guaranteed confidentiality from members' expected (not legally guaranteed) confidentiality.

**Q17: C** — Score of 9 (mild, not remitted); anhedonia and loss of meaning warrant behavioral activation + meaning-focused additions.

**Q18: B** — Somatic symptom cluster (fatigue, cognitive slowing, hair loss, weight gain) requires thyroid/medical rule-out before any psychiatric diagnosis.

**Q19: C** — Prior period of elevated mood, decreased sleep, and impulsive spending = possible hypomanic/manic episode → screen for bipolar.

**Q20: C** — Grief-triggered MDD with passive SI requires MDD diagnosis and treatment, including safety assessment.

### Questions 21–40

**Q21: B** — Relaxation addresses somatic symptoms; CBT for GAD also requires cognitive work on worry beliefs, intolerance of uncertainty, and avoidance.

**Q22: D** — Persistence + significant distress + functional impairment = phobia diagnosis. Disproportionate fear and avoidance alone are insufficient.

**Q23: B** — Blood-injection-injury phobia produces vasovagal response (not sympathetic arousal); applied tension is the evidence-based adaptation.

**Q24: C** — Three years of pervasive, uncontrollable multi-domain worry with insomnia and hyperarousal = GAD.

**Q25: B** — Stabilization precedes trauma processing; integrated treatment (not sequential) for co-occurring PTSD and SUD.

**Q26: B** — Criterion A (specific traumatic stressor) + specific symptom clusters (re-experiencing) = PTSD. Adjustment disorder has no Criterion A requirement.

**Q27: B** — Dissociation during processing = insufficient stabilization; return to Phase 1 before continuing Phase 2.

**Q28: B** — Indiscriminate approach to strangers following early caregiving disruption = DSED. RAD presents with inhibited, withdrawn attachment.

**Q29: B** — MI explores ambivalence from the client's own perspective; doesn't impose the counselor's agenda.

**Q30: B** — Open question evokes change talk; MI amplifies client's own motivation.

**Q31: B or C** — Moderate AUD, stable, motivated: IOP (2.1) or outpatient (1). Residential is not indicated.

**Q32: B** — Delirium tremens (visual hallucinations, disorientation, autonomic instability, 48–72 hours after last drink) = medical emergency.

**Q33: B** — Follow the pre-established crisis plan; reinforcing between-session contact with immediate callback would reinforce crisis behavior.

**Q34: B** — DBT goal: skills for emotion regulation, distress tolerance, interpersonal effectiveness, mindfulness → "life worth living."

**Q35: B** — Explore the meaning of the experience without defensiveness; the clinical content is the client's felt experience of the slight.

**Q36: C** — Avoidant PD: wants connection, fears rejection. Schizoid PD: genuinely indifferent to connection.

**Q37: B** — Must screen for prior elevated mood episodes before diagnosing MDD; missing bipolar history = wrong diagnosis + potentially harmful treatment.

**Q38: B** — Explore concerns + psychoeducation about relapse risk; collaborative conversation with prescriber.

**Q39: B** — Psychoeducation + comprehensive assessment (comorbidities) + multimodal planning is the first step for adult ADHD.

**Q40: B** — Stereotyped motor behavior + social communication deficit + echolalia = core ASD features.

### Questions 41–60

**Q41: B** — Acute-onset confusion in a previously oriented elder = delirium until proven otherwise = medical emergency.

**Q42: B** — ERP breaks the obsession-compulsion link by eliminating the ritual response, allowing anxiety to naturally reduce.

**Q43: B** — Excoriation disorder = Habit Reversal Training (HRT), not ERP.

**Q44: A** — Explore the medication concern non-confrontationally + coordinate with prescriber. The counselor does not argue with the delusional content.

**Q45: B** — Medical safety check (electrolytes) warranted with acute escalation in purging; then address the stressor in CBT.

**Q46: C** — BMI 14.5, HR 44, syncope = medical instability requiring hospitalization before outpatient therapy.

**Q47: B** — Specific plan + purchased means + lives alone + "soon" = high risk → full assessment + emergency psychiatric evaluation.

**Q48: C** — Reasonable suspicion = immediate mandatory report; counselor does not investigate; confidentiality does not protect abuse.

**Q49: B** — Prior suicide attempt is the single strongest predictor of future attempt.

**Q50: B** — Every suicidal expression in BPD requires current risk assessment; chronic suicidality does not eliminate the obligation.

**Q51: C** — Aggression + fire-setting + theft over 12+ months = Conduct Disorder (serious violation of others' rights).

**Q52: C** — Affirm medical clearances, acknowledge distress, begin CBT for health anxiety behaviors; additional specialist referral reinforces reassurance-seeking.

**Q53: B** — Phase 1 (stabilization) criteria must be assessed and met before Phase 2 (EMDR processing) begins in DID.

**Q54: B** — CBT-I (sleep restriction + stimulus control) with supported medication taper is the evidence-based approach.

**Q55: B** — Affirming, exploratory response is the appropriate first response to gender dysphoria disclosure; the counselor validates and assesses.

**Q56: C** — Reasonable suspicion of physical child abuse = immediate mandatory report. Client's request for confidentiality does not override reporting obligation.

**Q57: C** — Gifts: consider clinical significance, cultural context, and value; small termination gifts may be appropriate.

**Q58: B** — No communication with third parties without the client's written, specific authorization.

**Q59: B** — Affirm distress + maintain after-hours frame + provide crisis resources + bridge to next appointment.

**Q60: B** — Disclose to client + assess impact + consult + document; immediate termination not automatically required.
