The exam changed in 2022. Most prep didn't.

Today's NCMHCE hands you the diagnosis and asks what you'd do — assess, plan, intervene, stay in scope. It's a test of clinical decision-making, not a diagnosis quiz.

PassReady cases mirror that. Every decision you make carries a weight from −2 to +3, the same logic NBCC uses, so a confident-but-wrong call costs you exactly like it would on exam day.

How answers score
+3Best clinical action — what an examiner is looking for
+1Reasonable, but not the strongest move
0Neutral — neither helps nor harms
−2Harmful or out-of-scope — penalized like the real thing
